## Intro

The best ideas come from the worst moments.

---

## Personal Story

My junior year, I broke both bones in my forearm during practice and fainted from the injury. I was taken to Emory ER — and I waited over 12 hours for surgery. As a Chinese international student, it was my first real encounter with the American healthcare system. I didn't know what was happening, who to ask, or what came next. I was in pain, and completely lost in a system that wasn't built for me.

---

## Problem

That experience isn't unique to me. Every day, college students face medical situations with no guidance — wrong triage decisions, missed campus resources, no one translating the system for them. And underneath all of it: the fear of a bill they never saw coming.

For institutions, every one of those students is a blind spot — a situation they couldn't see, couldn't route, and couldn't support in time.

The care is there. The navigation isn't.

## Key Framing Decisions (2026-03-23)

### MedEase is a triage engine
The cleanest frame for the product. "Triage" covers all three pillars naturally:
- Triage to the right accommodation process
- Triage to the right level of clinical care
- Triage to the right financial/administrative resource

It connects directly back to the personal story (the ER wait was partly a triage failure) and is the spine the pitch should be built on.

### Product has three pillars
1. **Accommodation navigation** — DAS registration, documentation, renewals, exam accommodations, housing, professor communication, appeals
2. **Clinical guidance** — symptom triage, medication management, mental health resources
3. **Administrative / financial navigation** — billing, insurance, transportation

Plus crisis detection as a safety layer across all three.

### One-liner is not finalized
The original ("MedEase Companion is an AI-powered platform...") was rejected — too vague, tries to say everything. A replacement is pending until the pitch is fully locked.

### Institutional value angle
"Every student MedEase correctly triages is one who didn't show up at the wrong door."
Framed as value delivered, not problem diagnosed — institutions aren't broken, MedEase adds visibility and routing they don't currently have.

### ChatGPT answer (updated)
Key phrase: *"ChatGPT gives you a text answer. MedEase takes action."*
Differentiators: RAG on live institutional data (not stale training data) + functional agents that act, not just answer.
