# Pitch Mock — Personas & Questions

> Created: 2026-03-23
> Context: Student Founders Showcase prep

---

## Persona 1 — Skeptical Judge (healthcare investor background)

> "Healthcare AI assistants are a crowded space. What makes this different from just telling students to use ChatGPT or read the DAS website themselves?"

---

## Persona 2 — Compliance-Focused Judge (healthcare law / HIPAA background)

> "You're handling student health and disability information. Walk me through your HIPAA compliance story — do you have a BAA with your data vendors? Are you processing PHI through third-party LLM APIs, and if so, how do you handle that?"

---

## Persona 3 — Scale Skeptic (business school professor)

> "You're scoped to Emory DAS right now. That's a market of what, a few thousand students? How do you see this becoming a viable company? What's the expansion story after Emory?"

---

## Persona 4 — Student User (disability advocate, potential user)

> "I've used a lot of 'accessibility tools' that were clearly built by people who don't have disabilities. Did you actually talk to students with disabilities while building this? What did you learn?"

---

## Persona 5 — Technical Evaluator (ML engineer on the panel)

> "You mentioned RAG-based question answering over DAS documentation. What happens when the corpus is out of date or incomplete? How does the system behave when it doesn't have a good answer — does it hallucinate or does it gracefully fail?"

---

## Persona 6 — Risk-Averse Institutional Partner (Emory administrator / DAS office)

> "Has Emory officially endorsed or approved this tool? If a student relies on your system and gets wrong information about their accommodation eligibility, who's liable?"

---

## Persona 7 — Competitive Threat Questioner (fellow founder in the audience)

> "What stops Emory's IT department or the DAS office from just building this themselves, or licensing something from a larger EdTech vendor? What's your moat?"

---

## Persona 8 — Caregiver Angle Prober (parent in the audience)

> "You mention a caregiver dashboard. My kid is 19 — they're an adult. How are you handling consent for sharing their information with me as a parent? FERPA, HIPAA — how does that work?"

---

## Persona 9 — Business Model Questioner (MBA judge)

> "Who pays? Students? Emory directly? Are you thinking SaaS per institution? And are you far enough along to have had any revenue conversations with Emory?"

---

## Persona 10 — "What If It Goes Wrong" Judge (ethics-focused panelist)

> "What happens if a student in crisis — say, someone with a mental health condition — uses this chatbot and gets a response that's factually wrong or makes things worse? What safeguards do you have, and have you pressure-tested them?"
